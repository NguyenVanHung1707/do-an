    \Large{\textbf{ABSTRACT}}\\
\end{center}
\vspace{1cm}
\hspace*{1.5cm}The graduation thesis entitled "Building software to optimize the attendance and student management process" focuses on addressing major limitations in training management and online exam monitoring at educational institutions today. Traditional attendance methods waste class time and are prone to proxy attendance, whereas popular Learning Management Systems (LMS) like Moodle \cite{moodle_attendance_plugin} lack biometric support, and commercial proctoring software is expensive while requiring heavy server-side GPU resources. To resolve these issues, this thesis proposes a comprehensive, synchronized system consisting of a React-based Web dashboard \cite{react_docs2026} and a React Native mobile application \cite{react_native_docs2026}. The system operates on a microservice architecture \cite{newman2021buildingmicroservices} with a Spring Boot backend \cite{spring_boot_docs2026}, a PostgreSQL database \cite{postgresql_docs2026}, and Keycloak SSO centralized authentication \cite{keycloak2026}. The key contributions of this thesis include a group attendance solution (Group FaceID) utilizing RetinaFace \cite{Deng_2020_CVPR} and ArcFace \cite{Deng_2019_CVPR} models to process classroom photos in 1.5 to 2.5 seconds; a two-layer geofencing mechanism that blocks 100\% of mock location coordinates; and a real-time online exam monitoring (AI Proctoring) engine using MediaPipe FaceMesh \cite{Lugaresi2019MediaPipe}, solvePnP 3D pose estimation \cite{Lepetit2009EPnP}, and Gaze Tracking \cite{kaundinya2026inbrowser} streaming over WebSockets \cite{fette2011websocket} with a latency under 80 ms on standard CPUs. Experimental results with 150 students and 10 lecturers show stable system operation, attendance accuracy exceeding 95\%, 100\% block rate of simulated coordinates, and API response times under 150 ms, demonstrating the high practicality and feasibility of the proposed solution.
